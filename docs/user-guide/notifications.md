# Notifications

> Stay informed when your digital team mates finish responding, even when you are not looking at the app.

## What It Does

OpenMates has two types of notifications:

1. **In-app notifications** -- Cards that appear at the top of the app while you are using it.
2. **Push notifications** -- System-level alerts that appear in your browser or phone notification centre, even when the app is in the background.

## In-App Notifications

These appear as cards inside the app with a title, icon, and message. Current notification types:

- **Message completed** -- A digital team mate has finished responding to your message. You can reply directly from the notification card without navigating to the chat.
- **Auto logout** -- You were logged out due to session expiration. The notification suggests enabling "Stay logged in" at your next login.

Each notification has a close button to dismiss it.

## Push Notifications

Push notifications let you know about activity even when the app is in the background or minimised.

### Supported Platforms

| Platform | Support |
|----------|---------|
| Chrome, Edge, Firefox (desktop) | Full support |
| Safari (macOS) | Full support (Safari 16+) |
| Android (Chrome, Firefox) | Full support |
| iOS Safari | Must add to home screen as a web app first |

### Enabling Push Notifications

The first time you send a message, a banner appears in the chat asking if you want to enable notifications:

- Click **Enable Notifications** to turn them on.
- Click **Not Yet** to dismiss the banner for the rest of your session.
- The banner only appears once per chat and will not pester you.

You can also manage push notifications in **Settings > Chat > Notifications**.

### Notification Categories

You can control which push notifications you receive:

| Category | What it covers | Default |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **New Messages** | When a team mate finishes responding | On |
| **Server Events** | Connection issues, maintenance | On |
| **Software Updates** | New version available (admins only) | Off |

### iOS Setup

On iPhone and iPad, push notifications require an extra step:

1. Open the app in Safari.
2. Tap the Share button and select **Add to Home Screen**.
3. Open the app from your home screen.
4. Enable notifications when prompted.

## Tips

- You can reply to message notifications directly from the notification card -- no need to navigate to the chat first.
- If you denied notification permission in your browser, you will need to reset it in your browser settings before OpenMates can ask again.
- Push notifications are most useful when you send a complex request and switch to another app while waiting.
