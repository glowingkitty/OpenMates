# Operations Plan

## Key Questions to Answer

### Development Process
- How do we manage open source development and community contributions?
  > **Suggested Answer**: **Open Source Workflow**: GitHub-based development with community contributions; **Code Review**: All code changes require community review and approval; **Testing**: Comprehensive testing suite with community contributions; **Documentation**: Extensive documentation and examples for all features; **Release Cycle**: Monthly releases with community input and testing.

- What development methodologies and tools do we use?
  > **Suggested Answer**: Agile development with community input, GitHub for version control, automated testing and CI/CD, comprehensive documentation, and regular community feedback sessions.

- How do we ensure code quality and security in open source development?
  > **Suggested Answer**: Community code review, automated testing, security audits, regular security reviews, and community security contributions.

### Infrastructure & Technology
- What infrastructure do we need to support our platform?
  > **Suggested Answer**: **Cloud Infrastructure**: AWS/Azure with focus on sustainability and efficiency; **AI Models**: Open source models (Qwen, Llama) with custom fine-tuning; **Database**: PostgreSQL with Directus CMS for content management; **Caching**: Dragonfly (Redis-compatible) for performance optimization; **Security**: HashiCorp Vault for secrets management and encryption.

- How do we ensure scalability and reliability?
  > **Suggested Answer**: Cloud-based infrastructure, automated scaling, redundancy and failover, performance monitoring, and regular infrastructure reviews and optimization.

- What technology stack do we use and why?
  > **Suggested Answer**: Open source technologies for transparency, sustainability-focused infrastructure, privacy-first architecture, and community-driven technology choices.

### Team Structure & Hiring
- What team structure do we need to support our growth?
  > **Suggested Answer**: **Core Team (5-10 people)**: Full-time developers, community managers, operations; **Community Contributors (100+)**: Part-time contributors and maintainers; **Advisory Board**: Community-elected advisors and industry experts; **Key Roles**: CTO, Community Manager, Developer Relations, Operations Manager.

- How do we hire and retain talent in the open source space?
  > **Suggested Answer**: Competitive compensation, community recognition, flexible work arrangements, open source contribution opportunities, and alignment with values-driven mission.

- What roles are most critical for our success?
  > **Suggested Answer**: CTO for technical leadership, Community Manager for community engagement, Developer Relations for contributor support, and Operations Manager for business operations.

### Community Management
- How do we manage and grow our community?
  > **Suggested Answer**: **Communication**: Discord, Signal, Meetup groups for community engagement; **Events**: Regular meetups, hackathons, and community conferences; **Recognition**: Contributor rewards, recognition programs, and revenue sharing; **Governance**: Transparent decision-making and community voting processes.

- What tools and processes do we use for community engagement?
  > **Suggested Answer**: Discord for real-time communication, Signal for secure communication, Meetup groups for local events, regular community meetings, and transparent governance processes.

- How do we handle conflicts and maintain community health?
  > **Suggested Answer**: Clear conflict resolution processes, community moderation, transparent communication, and regular community health assessments.

### Quality Assurance
- How do we ensure quality across all our products and services?
  > **Suggested Answer**: **Testing**: Automated testing with community contributions; **Security**: Regular security audits and community security reviews; **Performance**: Continuous monitoring and optimization; **User Feedback**: Community feedback loops and user testing; **Documentation**: Comprehensive documentation and user guides.

- What testing and validation processes do we use?
  > **Suggested Answer**: Automated testing, community testing, user feedback, security audits, and regular quality assessments.

- How do we handle bugs and security issues in open source?
  > **Suggested Answer**: Community bug reporting, transparent security processes, regular security audits, community security contributions, and transparent communication about security issues.
